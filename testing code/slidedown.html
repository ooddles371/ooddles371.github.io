How are mutations introduced into a population?

DNA can fail to replicate properly or external factors can damage DNA such as radiation or chemicals.

How do cells sense their environment?

Protein systems embedded in the cell surface sense particular stimuli, two-component systems are an example of this!

What is a two-component system?

A protein, called a histidine kinase, is embedded in the cell surface. It is programmed to sense a stimulus and then decide whether or not to phosphorylate the response regulator. The response regulator is an enzyme that attaches to the DNA sequence. When phosphorylated, it promotes the transcription of the downstream gene.
